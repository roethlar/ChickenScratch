We were both kept after for fighting, and Mrs. Jillien, the vice
principle, who was a temporary from a school up in Rhode Island, thought
it would do us the most good to serve the community by helping deliver
hot macaroni and cheese to crusty old whores and miners who hadn't had
the sense to let the black lung take them. Mrs. Jillien got herself
raped that summer and never came back, and we all decided she took her
interloping ass back to Rhode Island. In any case, the food deliveries
were a real short-term thing.\
Me and Corn got on good, probably because both our daddies were fucks.
Mine drunk himself to death pretty early, so my damage wasn't nearly as
bad as Corn's. But I had enough of it to see it in him. The burn scar on
his cheek got purple-red when Corn got mad, and he always got mad when
some dumb shit looked at the scar. Corn kicked a lot of asses. He could
take a punch, on account of his daddy whooping him, and wasn't afraid of
anyone. Kids got the fuck out of Corn's way, which meant they got the
fuck out of my way, too.\
One Saturday, just before the end of our high school days, me and Corn
went up to Manchester. My cousin Billy knew an old army vet who
supplemented his government checks by selling moonshine, and we wanted
to celebrate the end of our classroom captivity.\
We were about a mile outside of Harlan when Corn said, "I got more to
celebrate than graduation."\
"You finally fuck that freshman marching band chick?" I asked. "What's
her name? Judy? Julie?"\
"It's Jody, and naw, I got word about my daddy. He's in jail. For
good."\
"Oh. Shit."\
"He killed some guy in a bar fight in Macon and they gave him life in
prison." Corn's voice was flat as the road.\
I pulled out our moonshine money and started flipping through the bills.
A couple were worn through. "I hope that old fuck ain't picky about his
cash. Looks like most of these wouldn't work in a vending machine."\
"He'll take it." Corn turned to me and flashed a smile. "Do you think
we'll have enough 'shine for Jody?"
